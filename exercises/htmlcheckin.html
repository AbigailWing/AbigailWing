Three Ways of Overcoming Writer's Block

1. Take a Break

Oftentimes, when we encounter writer's block, our first instinct is to power through, even if no new ideas come to us. We think that as long as we keep trying, eventually we will come up with a new idea. However, it usually helps to take a short break so that we can refocus with an open mind.


Here are some more benefits associated with taking short breaks: Benefits of Taking Breaks

2. Try the Pomodoro Technique

The Pomodoro technique is a time management strategy that can help you to significantly improve your productivity. It works like this:

  1. Set aside time in your day to write
  2. Set a timer for 25 minutes
  3. Write for 25 minutes with no distractions
  4. Take a 5 minute break before repeating the process
  5. Repeat the above steps for as long as it takes to complete the writing task you seek to accomplish

To learn more about this technique, click on the following link: Pomodoro Technique

3. Do Some Free Writing

A blank page can be intimidating to even the most seasoned writer. One way of pushing past the blank page barrier is to set aside 30 minutes to write down whatever comes to mind. Afterward, you can look through what you wrote and decide what you want to keep. Some of the benefits of this technique include:

  • Improving your writing skills
  • Building confidence in your writing abilities
  • Forming a writing habit
  • Allowing yourself to write without self-censoring
